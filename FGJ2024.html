Finnish Game Jam 2024.

  • January 30, 2024

Project description

The game was developed as part of the Finnish Game Jam 2024, held from January 26 to January 28, 2024. The team included Tarita Karhulahti and Riku Heino, we had 48 hours’ time to create a game that fit the predetermined theme which was a “make me laugh”. On the first day we started to plan our game and we decided to do Poor Little Goblins. The player's mission is to throw delicious food towards the Goblin King and fill his stomach to make him fall asleep. Time is crucial, as the fate of the little goblins hangs in the balance on the brink of laughter-induced chaos!

Description of the task

In the team my role involved modeling characters, specifically the little goblins and the king goblin, also taking charge of creating the cutscene in the game.

Description of methods

In the game, the player moves the character using keyboard WASD and mouse. Players must navigate the environment, avoiding a King Goblin while strategically hurling available food towards it. The primary goal is to feet the King Goblin into a state of slumber. However players should exercise caution, as speaking attracts the attention of the King Goblin leading to their capture and subsequent game over.

Above are the 3D models I made for the game.

Problems and solutions

Our main challenge was implementing a game with too many details, considering the size of our team. It would have been wiser to limit the scope of the game, especially considering that resource allocation should be carefully considered in a game jam event. Our team was relatively small, and we were missing one programmer, as time was spent on character modeling and creating cutscenes. Personally, I would have preferred to be more involved in programming, but unfortunately, the majority of my time was dedicated to character modeling. I learned the importance of efficiently allocating resources and prioritizing tasks in future projects.

Final assessment

I am pleased with our game that we developed during the game jam event. We had fun throughout the entire weekend and at the same time, I gained more knowledge about using the Unity timeline component and adopted a new rigging technique in Blender.
